Performance Marketing

What performance marketing means at Hale

Performance marketing isn't running ads and reporting on clicks. It's a connected system: media, creative, website experience, conversion paths, and measurement working together instead of competing for credit.

Spend more and the problem usually gets more expensive, not smaller. Better performance comes from better decisions across the system, not louder ones inside the ad platform.

That's why Hale looks past the dashboard. We look at the strategy behind the campaign, the quality of the traffic it brings, the message being tested, the page people land on, and whether the data actually supports a decision.

Built for brands that have outgrown "just run more ads"

Performance marketing problems rarely show up alone. They show up as one symptom of a bigger pressure.

Spend is climbing alongside growth, but nobody's sure if the campaigns are actually getting smarter or just bigger.

Paid media used to work. Now CAC is rising, creative is fatiguing, and another round of testing hasn't moved the number.

Paid media exists, but it's not consistently producing leads good enough to justify the spend, and leadership wants a straighter answer on why.

Growth Challenges

The problems performance marketing should actually solve

Nobody calls an agency because they want a "performance marketing agency." They call because something specific is going wrong.

  • Rising acquisition costs. Traffic's still coming. Each customer costs more than the last. Could be media efficiency, audience saturation, creative fatigue, or a conversion problem wearing a media-buying costume.

  • Poor return on ad spend. Spend is up, revenue isn't keeping pace. Rarely a platform problem alone — usually the offer, the creative, the landing page, or the tracking.

  • Weak conversion. The ads are doing their job. The site isn't doing its. Traffic arrives and leaves without a clear next step.

  • Unclear reporting. Nobody can confidently say what's working. Without that, every spend decision is a guess wearing a spreadsheet.

  • Disconnected campaigns. Paid, creative, SEO, email, and analytics all running separately. Performance gets harder to improve every time another channel joins the pile without joining the system.

How We Work

Audit. Strategy. Launch. Test. Optimize. Scale.

More budget only helps when the work underneath it can carry the weight. Here's the sequence — whether you're starting fresh or handing us campaigns that are already live.

  1. 1

    Audit

    Before anything changes, we look at what's actually running: account structure, tracking, creative, audiences, and where the budget is really going. Most paid media problems get found here.

  2. 2

    Strategy

    Clarify the business goal, audience, offer, funnel, and budget. Spend is the last decision, not the first.

  3. 3

    Launch

    Build the campaigns, accounts, and creative around the strategy, not a generic platform template.

  4. 4

    Test

    Creative, audiences, offers, and placements tested systematically, not occasionally. This is the stage most brands skip and the one that compounds the most.

  5. 5

    Optimize

    Use what the testing actually shows, not gut feel, to cut what's underperforming and put more weight behind what's working.

  6. 6

    Scale

    Grow spend behind the campaigns that have already earned it, with the landing pages and tracking built to handle the volume.

Performance marketing FAQs

What is performance marketing?
Marketing measured by outcomes: leads, purchases, conversions, revenue, return on ad spend. At Hale, that includes strategy, campaign management, creative testing, landing page alignment, and reporting — not just media buying.
What makes Hale's approach different?
We don't manage the ad platform in isolation. Paid media gets connected to strategy, creative, website experience, and measurement, because that's where most "media" problems actually live.
Which channels does Hale manage?
Google Ads, Meta, TikTok, LinkedIn, display, video, YouTube, and shopping campaigns, depending on the brand's goals and audience.
Is this only for ecommerce brands?
No. Ecommerce is a strong fit, but this also covers consumer brands, retailers, franchise and multi-location brands, and growth-stage companies that need marketing to produce measurable outcomes.
How do you measure success?
Depends on the goal. Usually revenue, leads, conversion rate, ROAS, CAC, traffic quality, or funnel performance — defined upfront, not after the fact.
Can you help if our ads are already running?
Yes. Most brands come to us mid-campaign. We audit what's live, find the gaps, fix tracking, and optimize from there instead of starting over.
What if our paid media problem is actually a website problem?
It usually is, at least partly. Weak landing pages, unclear messaging, slow load times, and broken tracking all show up disguised as a paid media problem. We check the whole path from click to conversion.
Do you offer paid media as a standalone service?
Yes. If you only need Google Ads, Meta Ads, or paid social managed well, that's exactly what this covers. Most brands end up wanting the connected version too once they see what's actually limiting paid performance, but starting with media alone is a fine place to start.
