SEO

What SEO means at Hale

SEO isn't a checklist of keywords and backlinks. It's the work of making a site genuinely easy to crawl, easy to understand, and worth ranking — for the actual people behind the search, not just the algorithm.

That means technical fixes that remove friction, content built around real search intent, and an authority profile that gives search engines a reason to trust the site. Skip any one of the three and the other two don't matter much.

Who It's For

Built for brands where SEO has stalled or never really started

  • Flat rankings. Pages haven't moved in months, and nobody's sure if it's a content problem, a technical one, or both.

  • Technical debt. Site speed, crawlability, or structure issues quietly capping how well anything else performs.

  • Thin content. Pages exist. They don't go deep enough to actually compete for the terms that matter.

  • Weak authority. Almost no backlink profile or third-party signals, which limits how much search engines trust the site.

  • Multi-location or local needs. Visibility that needs to hold up market by market, not just nationally.

Growth Challenges

The problems SEO should actually solve

  • Flat or declining rankings. Visibility that's stalled or slipping, usually for reasons further from the surface than most teams assume.

  • Technical issues suppressing visibility. Slow pages, broken crawlability, or structural problems quietly capping everything built on top of them.

  • Content that doesn't match intent. Pages exist for the keyword but don't actually answer what the searcher wanted.

  • Weak authority. Good content that doesn't rank because nothing else on the web vouches for the site.

  • Inconsistent local presence. Visibility that works in one market and disappears in the next.

SEO FAQs

What's the difference between this and Organic Performance?
SEO is the foundation: technical health, content, and authority. Organic Performance is the broader system that connects SEO to conversion, email, and retention. Most brands eventually want both, but SEO can stand on its own.
What's the difference between this and AI search optimization?
AI search optimization extends the same SEO fundamentals toward AI tools specifically — entity clarity, schema, structured answers. Strong SEO is the prerequisite for AI visibility, not a replacement for it.
Do you do link building?
Yes, through digital PR and earned coverage, not the low-quality directory submissions that can do more harm than good.
How long does SEO take to show results?
Months, not days, for anything competitive. Technical fixes can show signal faster. Content and authority building compound over a longer timeline.
Can you fix technical SEO without a full website rebuild?
Often, yes. Most technical issues can be resolved on the existing site. A rebuild is sometimes the right call, but it's rarely the first thing we recommend.
How do we get started?
A Performance Growth Audit, with a specific look at technical health, content gaps, and the current authority profile.
