Analytics & Measurement

What measurement means at Hale

Most reporting answers “what happened” without ever getting to “what do we do next.” That's the gap. A dashboard that shows traffic went up isn't useful if nobody can say why, or what to do with that information.

Hale builds measurement around the decisions it's supposed to support, not around whatever the platform happens to track by default.

Who It's For

Built for brands that can't trust their own data

  • Unclear reporting. Reports get sent. Nobody's totally sure what to do with them once they arrive.

  • Data nobody trusts. Numbers don't match across tools, or the team has stopped fully believing the dashboard.

  • Spending across channels. Paid, organic, email, and social all running, with no clear way to see what's actually contributing.

  • No link to revenue. Marketing activity is visible. Whether it's actually driving the business forward isn't.

Growth Challenges

The problems measurement should actually solve

  • Inaccurate conversion tracking. The numbers don't reflect what's really happening, so every decision built on them is a little bit wrong.

  • Confusing reports. Data exists. Nobody can read it fast enough to actually use it.

  • Channel performance uncertainty. No clear answer on which channels are actually earning their budget.

  • Weak attribution. Credit for a conversion gets assigned to whatever touched it last, which usually isn't the real story.

  • No visibility into funnel drop-off. Traffic comes in, conversions happen, and the gap between the two is a black box.

Analytics and measurement FAQs

Do you set up GA4 from scratch, or fix what's already there?
Both. Some brands need a full setup. Others have GA4 running but configured poorly, which is just as common and just as costly.
What's the difference between tracking and reporting?
Tracking is the data collection underneath. Reporting is what gets built on top of it. Bad tracking makes good reporting impossible, so we always start there.
Can you fix attribution if it's been wrong for a while?
Yes, though historical data stays historical. We can fix it going forward and help interpret what the older data can and can't tell you.
How often should reporting actually happen?
Depends on the business, but more frequent isn't always better. We build the cadence around when decisions actually get made, not an arbitrary schedule.
Do you build dashboards in tools we already use?
Yes. We work within GA4, Looker Studio, and most major reporting platforms rather than forcing a new tool into the stack.
How do we get started?
A Performance Growth Audit, which includes a look at current tracking and reporting gaps as part of the broader review.
