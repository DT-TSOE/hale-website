Food & Beverage

Marketing for brands that live and die by trial and repeat purchase

Food and beverage brands face a category problem most others don't: someone has to actually taste the product before brand loyalty means anything. That makes trial the real first conversion, not the sale itself.

Hale helps food and beverage brands build the demand that earns trial, the retail and ecommerce presence that captures the sale, and the retention strategy that turns a first purchase into a habit.

Growth Challenges

The pressure points specific to food and beverage

  • Product discovery. Standing out in a category where shelf space and search results are both crowded with similar products.

  • Retail and ecommerce growth. Managing demand across physical retail, direct-to-consumer, and everything in between.

  • Seasonal campaigns. Demand that shifts with holidays, weather, and occasions, in ways a generic content calendar doesn't account for.

  • Brand differentiation. Explaining why this product, specifically, when the shelf is full of close substitutes.

  • Repeat purchase. Winning trial is one problem. Earning a place in someone's regular routine is a different one entirely.

  • Measurement across channels. Connecting retail distribution, ecommerce, and direct sales into one picture instead of three.

Food and beverage marketing FAQs

Do you work with brands that sell through retail, ecommerce, or both?
Yes. Most food and beverage brands need both working together, and that's where the measurement usually gets harder, not easier.
Can you help with seasonal or occasion-based campaigns specifically?
Yes. Demand in this category moves with holidays, seasons, and occasions, and campaign planning needs to move with it.
How do you approach a crowded category with similar competitor products?
Usually through sharper positioning and more specific targeting, rather than competing purely on volume or price.
Do you help with subscription or repeat-purchase models?
Yes. Retention and lifecycle marketing matter as much as acquisition in this category, sometimes more.
Can you support both national campaigns and smaller, local activations?
Yes, depending on the brand's footprint and goals.
