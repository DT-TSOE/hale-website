Retail

Performance marketing for retailers balancing digital and in-store growth

Retail is harder to measure than pure ecommerce — not because the marketing is more complex, but because the outcome splits across two channels that rarely talk to each other. A campaign drives a store visit. A search drives an online sale. Most reporting only sees one half of that story.

Hale builds marketing and measurement for retailers around the customer's actual path, not around which channel happens to be easiest to track.

Growth Challenges

The pressure points retail feels differently than pure ecommerce

  • Connecting online and offline performance. A digital campaign that drives store visits looks like it underperformed online, when it actually worked exactly as intended.

  • Competing in paid search and shopping. Crowded categories where every competitor is bidding on the same terms and the same product feeds.

  • Improving local visibility. Showing up for “near me” and location-specific searches across every market the brand operates in.

  • Increasing conversion. Both online and in-store, where friction shows up differently but costs the same.

  • Measuring channel impact. Knowing which channel actually deserves credit when the customer journey spans both.

Outcomes

What better retail marketing actually looks like

  • Improved traffic quality. Visitors, online and in-store, who are more likely to convert.

  • Stronger product and category visibility. Better presence in shopping feeds and search for the categories that matter most.

Retail marketing FAQs

Can you actually connect online campaigns to in-store sales?
We build toward that connection wherever the tracking allows it — store visit conversions, location-based reporting, and consistent measurement across both channels. It's rarely perfect, but it's far better than treating them as unrelated.
Do you handle local SEO for multi-location retailers?
Yes, including Google Business Profile management and citation consistency across every market the brand operates in.
Can you help with seasonal and promotional campaigns specifically?
Yes. Retail demand moves on its own calendar, and campaign planning needs to move with it, not against it.
Do you manage shopping feeds and product listings?
Yes, as part of paid media and SEO working together, since product visibility depends on both.
What if our online and in-store teams use different reporting today?
That's common. Part of this work is building a shared measurement view so both teams are looking at the same picture.
How do we get started?
A Performance Growth Audit, scoped to look at both digital and in-store performance together.
