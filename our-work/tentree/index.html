The Challenge

What we were up against

tentree had a clear mission and a loyal customer base, but growth had stalled. Return on ad spend had flattened, acquisition costs were climbing, and the media mix lacked the flexibility to adapt.

They didn't need a new mission. They needed a new engine.

What We Did

How we rebuilt the engine

  1. 1

    Built a smarter media framework

    We rebuilt paid media from the ground up. Spend was aligned to high-value customer behaviours, budgets shifted to improve the marketing efficiency ratio, and search, social, and programmatic were brought together under one growth strategy.

  2. 2

    Simplified the creative loop

    Polished assets were replaced with insight-driven content built around authenticity, sustainability, and storytelling. Creative testing was tightened and tied directly to performance goals.

  3. 3

    Connected purpose to purchase

    We introduced a performance-owned approach across the site. Product pages were optimized, SEO was refreshed, and conversion-rate experiments helped turn traffic into actual tree planters.

Why it worked

tentree didn't need a new story — they needed it to perform better. We tightened the focus, simplified the process, and made the mission work harder.

The result was more than improved performance. It was sustained momentum, delivered cleanly and efficiently.

Paid social and search performance improved without increasing budget. Creative cycles dropped from two weeks to under five days, and media, CRO, and SEO started working together as one system.
