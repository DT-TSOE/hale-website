The Challenge

What we were up against

Saje had the brand and the loyalty, but media efficiency had stalled, creative was underdelivering, and growth was coming at too high a cost.

The internal team had the vision. They needed a partner to drive velocity and rebuild the digital foundation.

What We Did

How we rebuilt the engine

  1. 1

    Rebuilt the media engine

    We restructured campaigns around customer behaviour, not product. Audiences were refined, KPIs were reset, and we created real feedback loops between media and creative.

  2. 2

    Flipped the creative process

    We moved away from polished assets toward lean, insight-led iterations. Instead of asking what looked good, we asked what performed.

  3. 3

    Made every dollar work harder

    Budgets were aligned to outcomes and spend was pushed toward the best return. Real-time changes helped reduce cost and lift return. We also navigated a site split from a single .com to separate .com and .ca properties while maintaining organic and paid performance, and built their paid-media reporting infrastructure.

Why it worked

Saje didn't need more platforms or polished decks. They needed focus. Together we reset the foundation, clarified the message, tightened execution, and picked up speed.

Performance stopped being a report card. It became the mindset.

Creative testing dropped from 14 days to under 6. The ROAS lift held with spend steady, and a new product category reached break-even in under 30 days.
